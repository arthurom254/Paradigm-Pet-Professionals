All About Dogs

Discover how to care for your dog at every stage of their life, from feeding and exercise to grooming, vaccinations, and more!

Why Dogs Make Great Companions

The domestic dog is an extremely social animal with well over 300 breeds recognized by the World Canine Organization. Studies show that adopting a dog brings social and medical benefits to owners, including reduced loneliness, lower blood pressure, and improved lipid profiles.

Diet & Exercise

From puppies to adults, dogs’ diet needs evolve over time. Puppies need frequent meals, while adult dogs thrive on one meal a day. Ensure your dog stays hydrated to avoid health issues. Regular exercise is also essential. Aim for 30–120 minutes of physical activity depending on your dog’s size and breed.

Exercise doesn’t have to be strenuous—brisk walks work just fine. Invest in age-appropriate toys that keep your dog active, but ensure they don’t pose any choking hazards.

Grooming & Handling

Grooming is key to keeping your dog healthy. Brush your dog frequently to reduce shedding and avoid matted fur. Check for ticks and fleas, and bathe them with dog-safe soap. Be gentle when handling your dog—whether big or small, always support them properly to avoid injury.

Vaccinations & Medications

Your veterinarian will recommend core vaccines for diseases like hepatitis, rabies, and parvovirus. Based on your dog’s environment, other vaccines like Bordetella, Borrelia, and Leptospira may also be necessary. Always consult your vet about your dog’s vaccination and medication needs.
